## üéØ How to Get Best Results

### **For Photography:**

1. **Good Lighting is Critical**
   - Take photos in bright, even lighting
   - Avoid harsh shadows or dark corners
   - Natural daylight works best

2. **Clear, Unobstructed Walls**
   - Show walls clearly without too much furniture blocking them
   - Straight-on angles work better than extreme perspectives
   - Full wall surfaces give better results

3. **Image Quality**
   - Use higher resolution photos when possible
   - Avoid blurry or pixelated images
   - Close-ups of walls work better than wide room shots

### **For Color Selection:**

1. **Click on Uniform Areas**
   - Click in the middle of a wall, not near edges
   - Avoid clicking on shadows or highlights
   - Stay away from wall/trim boundaries

2. **Work with Similar Colors**
   - The tool works best when the wall is a fairly uniform color
   - Textured or patterned walls may have inconsistent results
   - Solid-colored walls = best results

3. **Use Surface Type Selector**
   - Switch to "Trim & Doors" before clicking trim
   - Switch to "Ceiling" before clicking ceiling
   - This helps you track what you're painting

## ‚ö†Ô∏è Current Limitations

### **How the Tool Works:**
The app uses **flood fill technology** (like a "magic wand" in photo editing software). When you click on a surface, it selects all connected pixels that are similar in color to where you clicked.

### **Known Limitations:**

1. **Can't Distinguish Objects**
   - The tool doesn't "know" what's a wall vs. furniture vs. floor
   - It only sees colors and selects similar-colored areas
   - If your furniture is the same color as your wall, it might get painted too

2. **Works Best On:**
   - ‚úÖ Plain, solid-colored walls
   - ‚úÖ Walls with consistent lighting
   - ‚úÖ Well-lit, clear photos
   - ‚úÖ Uniform surfaces

3. **Challenging Scenarios:**
   - ‚ùå Walls with heavy shadows
   - ‚ùå Patterned wallpaper or textured walls
   - ‚ùå Furniture touching or same color as walls
   - ‚ùå Poor lighting or dark photos
   - ‚ùå Wood floors that are similar color to walls

### **Workaround Tips:**

1. **Multiple Clicks**
   - If only part of the wall got painted, click again on an unpainted section
   - You can click multiple times to fill in missed areas
   - Use the "Reset" button to start over if needed

2. **Accept Some Imperfection**
   - The tool is for quick visualization, not photo-realistic rendering
   - Small imperfections are normal
   - Focus on getting the overall color feel right

3. **Best Use Cases**
   - Quick color decision-making
   - Comparing multiple color options
   - Getting a general idea of how a color looks
   - Not meant to replace professional color samples

## üîÆ Future Enhancements (Beyond Current Scope)

To truly paint **only** walls and avoid furniture/floors would require:
- **AI Image Segmentation** - Machine learning to identify object types
- **3D Room Modeling** - Understanding spatial relationships
- **Object Recognition** - Knowing what's a wall vs. what's a chair

These are advanced features that would require:
- Significant development time
- Cloud-based AI processing (expensive)
- Slower response times (5-10 seconds vs. current <2 seconds)

## üí° Recommended Workflow

1. **Upload a good quality photo** with clear walls
2. **Click in the center of a wall** (not near edges)
3. **If it selects unwanted areas:**
   - Click "Reset"
   - Try clicking a different spot on the wall
   - Take a new photo with better lighting/angle
4. **Use it for color comparison** rather than perfect accuracy
5. **Get the SKU numbers** and get actual paint samples for final decision
